# PRD.md — Metz & Partner Website Product Brief

This document defines business truth, website goals, audience context, and non-negotiable honesty rules.

It does **not** define a fixed homepage structure, fixed copy, or fixed UI solutions.

---

### Primary Goal
Generate more qualified inbound inquiries.

- Increase perceived quality and distinctiveness of Metz & Partner

## 4. Audience Context

The audience should be treated as broad enough to allow strong creative freedom, but grounded enough to keep the work commercially useful.

### Audience Reality
- The site should be legible and persuasive for real business buyers, not just designers.
- The audience may include regional businesses, service businesses, founders, and broader clients beyond the immediate local market.
- Regional presence may act as a trust signal, but should not reduce the brand to a small local-provider image.

### Buyer Psychology
Likely buyers respond to:
- visible quality
- confidence without fake posturing
- real examples of work
- clarity of offer
- a sense that Metz & Partner has stronger taste and sharper execution than generic providers

Likely buyers are turned off by:
- generic agency language
- templated SaaS aesthetics
- weak or fake proof
- too much text
- work that feels junior, cheap, or AI-generated

---

## 5. Offer Framing

The website should clearly position Metz & Partner around website work only.

### Core Public Offer
**Webdesign & Entwicklung** — website strategy, design, and development.

This is the only public service. Full stop.

### Explicitly excluded from the site
- SEO packages
- Branding / visual identity packages
- Retainers or ongoing support contracts
- Any “full-service agency” positioning

### Offer Principles
- The site should present Metz & Partner as more than “just implementation” — strategy, concept, and craft are part of the offer.
- The site should not collapse into vague “full-service agency” language.
- How Webdesign & Entwicklung is broken down, structured, or presented on the site is a creative decision — Claude has full autonomy here.
- The entry price point is under €1,000 — do not use language that implies €10,000+ budgets. Signal exceptional quality-to-value ratio instead.

### Homepage Offer Application
For the homepage, the services / what-you-get section should present **one public offer only**.

Default section framing:
- `Was Sie bekommen`
- one focused offer: `Webdesign & Entwicklung`
- internal module logic: `Design`, `Entwicklung`, `Launch & Übergabe`

The section should clarify and strengthen the offer.
It should not expand the business into multiple visible public services.

## 10. Preferred Homepage Narrative

The homepage will usually work best when structured in this order:

1. Hero
2. Proof / Selected Work
3. Why Metz & Partner
4. Services / What You Get
5. Process
6. Contact
7. Footer

This is the preferred conversion structure for Metz & Partner at the current stage.

It should be treated as a strong strategic default, not as rigid page law.
Sections may be merged, reduced, expanded, or replaced if a clearly stronger solution emerges.

What matters most is the logic of the sequence:
- immediate relevance
- visible proof
- clear differentiation
- concrete offer
- risk reduction
- conversion
- clean close

For the homepage offer section specifically:
- default framing: `Was Sie bekommen`
- default structure: one dominant offer section, not a multi-service overview
- default internal logic: `Design`, `Entwicklung`, `Launch & Übergabe`
